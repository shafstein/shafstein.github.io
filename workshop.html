Workshop on Algorithms for Dynamical Systems and Lyapunov Functions

Reykjavík 17.7-19.7.2013

Program and Abstracts

Map

Talks of some of the participants can be downloaded as pdf below


Many systems in applications can be viewed as dynamical systems, and researchers working in dynamical systems come from applied mathematics, engineering, informatics or other sciences. Algorithms for dynamical systems use a variety of mathematical methods from areas such as numerics, approximation theory, optimization, graph theory and many more. Hence, the experts in central areas of dynamical systems, such as Lyapunov functions, are distributed amongst the different disciplines in science, but also between various mathematical areas. The exchange between these different researchers has not been established by usual conferences, which are normally restricted to one mathematical area. This workshop aims to overcome this lack of communication by inviting researchers from different disciplines and mathematical areas to exchange methods and ideas, and to facilitate new collaborations. A particular emphasis is on the active participation of young researchers, to provide them with a forum of presenting their own research and to learn about new areas.
